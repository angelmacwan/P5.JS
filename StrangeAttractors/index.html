Strange Attractors

The term 'Strange Attractor' is used to describe an attractor
(a region or shape to which points are 'pulled' as the result of a certain process)
that displays sensitive dependence on initial conditions
(that is, points which are initially close on the attractor become exponentially separated
with time). The most famous strange attractor is undoubtedly the Lorenz attractor
- a three dimensional object whose body plan resembles a butterfly or a mask.
